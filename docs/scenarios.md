# Scenario Guide

MMT Economy Lab includes six built-in scenarios. Each one is designed to isolate a different macro mechanism while keeping the same model structure underneath.

Use this guide as a learning path. Start with the baseline, then move one scenario at a time and ask which constraint becomes binding.

After changing a scenario or moving policy knobs, use **Share** to copy a URL that preserves the current tab, period, language, scenario, and custom policy values. This makes it easier to compare interpretations with someone else using the same experiment state.

## 1. Sovereign Stabilizer

**Theme:** fiscal support plus job guarantee buffer

This is the baseline scenario. It represents a sovereign-currency economy with active public spending, moderate taxation, and a relatively strong job guarantee.

Watch:

- Private net financial assets
- Unemployment
- Capacity use
- Fiscal deficit
- Social actor map for workers and the state

Learning question:

```text
When real resources are still available, how does a fiscal deficit show up as private-sector income and net financial assets?
```

## 2. Austerity Demand Gap

**Theme:** low deficit plus high unemployment

This scenario lowers public spending, raises taxes, weakens the job guarantee, and makes credit impulse slightly negative.

Watch:

- GDP path
- Unemployment
- Private balance
- Bank credit creation
- Worker household risk exposure

Learning question:

```text
If the government tries to reduce its deficit while the private sector is not expanding credit, where does demand come from?
```

## 3. Real Resource Limit

**Theme:** deficit expansion plus supply bottleneck

This scenario combines high spending and job guarantee support with weak productivity and high energy costs.

Watch:

- Capacity use
- Inflation
- Real output versus capacity
- Firm risk exposure
- State risk exposure

Learning question:

```text
When does public spending stop increasing real output and start expressing itself as inflation pressure?
```

## 4. Private Credit Cycle

**Theme:** bank expansion plus asset prices

This scenario uses a high credit impulse, low policy rate, and moderate fiscal support. It is designed to show bank credit as a source of demand and financial fragility.

Watch:

- Bank loans
- Household and firm deposits
- Asset price index
- Credit creation
- Financial stability score

Learning question:

```text
How can private credit expansion make the economy look strong while increasing balance-sheet risk?
```

## 5. External Constraint

**Theme:** import dependence plus exchange-rate pass-through

This scenario raises import dependence, lowers export demand, and adds energy pressure. It is useful for thinking about open economies where fiscal expansion leaks abroad.

Watch:

- Imports and exports
- Current account
- Exchange-rate index
- Foreign sector balance
- Import gap gauge

Learning question:

```text
How does import dependence change the domestic impact of fiscal policy?
```

## 6. Automation Platform Economy

**Theme:** high output plus distribution pressure

This scenario raises productivity and automation while keeping redistribution relatively weak. It is designed to show that high output does not automatically mean broad gains.

Watch:

- Wage share
- Inequality
- Asset price index
- Platform / automation capital card
- Distribution score

Learning question:

```text
Can productivity growth and automation raise capacity while weakening labor's share of income?
```

## Suggested Learning Path

1. Run **Sovereign Stabilizer** and observe the accounting identity.
2. Switch to **Austerity Demand Gap** and compare unemployment and private balance.
3. Switch to **Real Resource Limit** and identify the inflation constraint.
4. Switch to **Private Credit Cycle** and follow loans, deposits, and asset prices.
5. Switch to **External Constraint** and watch the current account.
6. Switch to **Automation Platform Economy** and compare output with distribution.
7. Open **Policy Compare** and include the current slider policy.
8. Adjust one knob at a time and explain the result before moving another knob.

## How to Read the App

- The **Overview** tab shows the headline macro path.
- The **Balances** tab shows accounting closure.
- The **Bank Credit** tab shows how loans and deposits evolve.
- The **Real Resources** tab shows whether demand is hitting supply limits.
- The **Modern Society** tab shows distribution, asset prices, and social actors.
- The **Policy Compare** tab turns scenarios into side-by-side policy experiments.

The strongest use of the app is not to find the "best" scenario. It is to understand which constraint is doing the work.
